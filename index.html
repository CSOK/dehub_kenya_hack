  • FAQ

    Can I attend?

    If you are a passionate programmer from within the country then yes you are invited to join us in this year's DeHUB Kenya Hack.

    How does registration work?

    Registration is open until June 01st, 11:59 pm EAT! Admissions are to the event though, once at the event one has to pitch and the idea vetted in order to take part. If your idea is declined then you can join another team.

    Does this cost money?

    Nope! Admission is free and includes meals, drinks, snacks, overnight hosting, workshops, swag, and a memorable experience (if we do say so ourselves)!

    What will I eat?

    We’ll provide food for all meals from Friday's Supper to Sunday’s lunch. If you have dietary restrictions, we’ll try our best to accommodate them. Of course, we’ll also have an abundance of snacks throughout the hacking period.

    Where will I sleep?

    Hacking all night is fun, but you can also get some rest when you need it! If you indicate in your confirmation form, we can match you with an DeKUT student who will provide places to sleep and shower. 😴😴😴

    How do I get there?

    If you are travelling from Nairobi, boader a matatu to Nyeri. Once you are in Nyeri town take a matatu(Marked Dedan Kimathi University) towards Nanyuki (7km from town) lies Dedan Kimathi University of Technology.

    What should I bring?

    Bring your laptop, a national ID card or an original issued school ID card, dont forget your idea ;).

    How do teams work?

    Teams are at most 4 people. You can select teammates when you register, or you can register individually without specified teammates. We’ll run the lottery by teams, so rest assured you’ll be able to hack with your friends if that’s what you prefer!

    What if I don’t have a team?

    If you don’t have a team now, that’s okay! We’ll have team formation and ideation events geared towards helping you find people to work with.

    What if I’m a DeKUT student?

    If you register by June 07th and confirm on June 08th, you will be guaranteed a spot at DeHUB Kenya Hack. Otherwise, you can try for walk-in admission the day of the event.

    On a team with DeKUT and non-DeKUT hackers, are the non-DeKUT hackers automatically admitted?

    No. The DeKUT students will be guaranteed a spot at DeHUB Kenya Hack, while the rest of the team will be admitted as a unit via our admission process.

    What about hardware hacks?

    We’ll provide some hardware such as Arduinos, Pebbles, Myos—the usual hacker essentials and more. Let us know what you’d like to hack on in our registration form.

    Where’s the schedule?

    We’ll release a more detailed schedule after first week of June. For now, know that we’re planning for check-in to start Saturday at 8 am and for closing ceremony to end Sunday at 4 pm.

    What are factions?

    We created factions to encourage hackers to have fun and interact with each other outside of normal teams when you’re not working on your projects. The Red Faction and Blue Faction are hackathon-wide groups that collectively complete challenges at DeHUB Kenya Hack 2018 for points. The faction with the most points will win and get a surprise upon the conclusion of the hackathon.

    How do I choose a faction?

    You can choose your faction when you register and confirm your spot!

    Can team members belong to different factions?

    Yes, factions do not limit your forming of individual teams.
